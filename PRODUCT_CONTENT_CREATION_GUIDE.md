# Product Content Creation Guide

**Purpose:** Create SEO-optimized, persona-targeted product descriptions for organic foods, Ayurvedic products, and supplements. Output is Markdown only—no code changes.

**Audience:** Health-conscious European consumers (DACH focus). German copy unless otherwise specified.

---

## 1. Target Personas

| Persona | Profile | Priorities |
|--------|---------|------------|
| **Holistic Health Seekers** (30s–40s women) | Whole-body wellness, yoga, nutrition, sustainability | Rituals, quality, natural origins |
| **Stressed Professionals** | Busy, time-poor | Quick solutions for stress, sleep, cognitive function |
| **Natural Moms & Dads** | Family-first | Safety, purity, chemical-free options |
| **Green Beauty Enthusiasts** | Natural beauty, visible results | Instagram-worthy, sensory experience |
| **Traditionalists 50+** | Trust in tradition | Joint health, digestion, sleep, time-tested remedies |

---

## 2. Product Categories & Angles

- **Adaptogens:** Stress-reduction, immunity, practical daily use
- **Skincare:** Traditional wisdom + modern effectiveness, sensory appeal
- **Female Wellness:** Empowering language, cycle-specific benefits
- **Detox / Gut Health:** Seasonal wellness, digestive support
- **Essential Oils:** Dosha-specific benefits, practical applications

## 3. Processing Original Content

### Rules

- **Preserve separators:** Keep `|` in property lists exactly as in the original.
- **Ignore:** Pricing, variant info (e.g. €8,99 – €23,99), SKUs.
- **Use:** Product name, quality cues, ingredients, format. Enhance and expand into full content.

### Input Example

```
BIO Ashwagandha Pulver | Premium-Qualität | Aus der Ashwagandha-Wurzel (Withania somnifera) €8,99 – €23,99*
```

### Output Structure

See **Output Format** (Section 6) and **Template** (Section 8).

---

## 4. Content Sections

### 4.1 Long-Form Description (300–500 words)

- Target **1–2 primary personas** explicitly.
- Explain **Ayurvedic context** (traditional use, plant story) in accessible language.
- Focus on **2–3 main benefits**; avoid information overload.
- Balance **tradition + modern lifestyle** (e.g. adaptogens for stressful workdays).
- **No medical claims.** Use “traditionell geschätzt”, “unterstützt”, “begleitet” etc.
- Optional: brief reference to **research** or “wissenschaftlich betrachtet” where appropriate.

### 4.2 Property List (Produkteigenschaften)

- Keep **original structure** and **`|`** separators.
- Improve **readability** (consistent spacing, clear categories).
- No prices or variants.

### 4.3 Advantage Paragraph (80–100 words)

- Link **Ayurveda** to **modern life** (stress, sleep, routines).
- Emphasise **premium quality**, **purity**, **sustainability** where relevant.
- Subtle **social proof** (e.g. “von vielen geschätzt”, “beliebt bei …”) without overclaiming.

## 5. SEO Elements

| Element | Spec | Notes |
|--------|------|-------|
| **Title** | 50–60 chars | Primary keyword, clear benefit |
| **Description** | 120–155 chars | Pain point + benefit + soft CTA |
| **Keywords** | 5–7 | Product terms, benefits, Ayurvedic terms, problem–solution, long-tail |

## 7. Addressing Barriers

| Barrier | Approach |
|--------|----------|
| **Price sensitivity** | Justify premium: certifications (Bio, etc.), potency, sourcing, transparency. |
| **Efficacy skepticism** | Balance tradition with “research”, “Studien”, “wissenschaftlich begleitet” — still no medical claims. |
| **Information overload** | Clear structure, 2–3 primary benefits, scannable lists. |
| **Sensory concerns** | Address taste/smell explicitly; suggest uses (Smoothies, Golden Milk, etc.). |

---

## 8. Tone & Compliance

- **Knowledgeable yet accessible.** No jargon without brief explanation.
- **Scientific yet emotional.** Balance “traditionell” / “wissenschaftlich” with “Wohlbefinden”, “Ruhe”, “Leichtigkeit”.
- **No medical claims.** No “heilt”, “behandelt”, “lindert Erkrankungen”. Use “unterstützt”, “begleitet”, “traditionell geschätzt”.
- **Transparency:** Purity, sourcing, sustainability. Optional subtle social proof.

---

## 9. Market Notes

- **Transparency**, **ingredient purity**, and **sustainability** strongly influence purchase.
- **Social media** influences 74%+ of purchases; include subtle, shareable angles (rituals, aesthetics, dosha).
- **EU / DACH:** Comply with local advertising and health-claim regulations.
